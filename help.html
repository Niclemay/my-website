Technical Indicators Help

This page explains the indicators used in the Market Indicators Report.

Generated on: 2025-10-05 20:52:17

RSI (Relative Strength Index)

Definition: RSI measures the speed and change of price movements. It oscillates between 0 and 100.

Calculation: Based on the average gains vs losses over the last 14 periods.

Buy Signal: RSI is below 30–35 (oversold condition).

MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)

Definition: MACD shows the relationship between two moving averages (usually 12-day EMA and 26-day EMA).

Calculation: MACD = (12-day EMA − 26-day EMA). A 9-day EMA of MACD is the Signal line. The histogram shows the difference between them.

Buy Signal: MACD line crosses above the Signal line.

Moving Averages (MA20 & MA50)

Definition: A moving average smooths price data to identify trends over time.

Calculation: Average of the last N closing prices (20-day and 50-day in this script).

Buy Signal: Short-term MA (MA20) crosses above long-term MA (MA50).

Volume

Definition: Volume measures how many shares/contracts were traded during a period.

Calculation: Compare current volume to the 20-day average volume.

Buy Signal: Current volume is more than 1.5x the 20-day average (indicating strong confirmation of a move).

Bollinger Bands

Definition: Bollinger Bands measure volatility and consist of a middle band (20-day SMA) and two bands above/below (±2 standard deviations).

Calculation: Upper Band = SMA + (2 × Std Dev), Lower Band = SMA − (2 × Std Dev).

Buy Signal: Price closes below the Lower Band (potential oversold condition).

ADX (Average Directional Index)

Definition: ADX measures the strength of a trend (not its direction).

Calculation: Based on the difference between +DI and −DI lines, smoothed over time.

Buy Signal: +DI crosses above −DI AND ADX > 20 (indicating a strong trend).
