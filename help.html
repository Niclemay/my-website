RSI (Relative Strength Index)

Definition: Measures speed and magnitude of price movements to identify overbought/oversold conditions (0–100).

Calculation: RSI = 100 - (100 / (1 + RS)), where RS = average gains / average losses over 14 periods.

Swing trading logic:

  • RSI < 35 → oversold → potential buy
  • RSI > 65 → overbought → potential sell

MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)

Definition: Momentum indicator comparing short-term EMA (12-day) to long-term EMA (26-day).

Buy Signal: MACD line crosses above Signal line → bullish momentum.

Sell Signal: MACD line crosses below Signal line → bearish momentum.

Moving Averages (MA20 & MA50)

Definition: Simple moving averages smooth price data to identify trends.

Buy Signal: MA20 crosses above MA50 (Golden Cross) → potential uptrend.

Sell Signal: MA20 crosses below MA50 (Death Cross) → potential downtrend.

Volume

Definition: Number of shares/contracts traded daily.

Logic: Volume spikes (> 1.5 × 20-day average) confirm trends. Can signal continuation or reversal.

OBV (On-Balance Volume)

Definition: A cumulative indicator that measures buying and selling pressure by adding volume on up days and subtracting it on down days.

Calculation:

  • If Close > Previous Close → OBV = Previous OBV + Volume
  • If Close < Previous Close → OBV = Previous OBV − Volume
  • If Close = Previous Close → OBV = Previous OBV (unchanged)

Swing trading logic:

  • Rising OBV while price is flat → accumulation → potential breakout.
  • Falling OBV while price is flat → distribution → potential breakdown.
  • Divergence (price up, OBV down) → weakening trend → caution signal.
  • OBV confirming price trend → strong momentum → trend continuation likely.

Bollinger Bands

Definition: 20-day SMA (middle) ± 2 standard deviations (upper & lower bands).

Buy Signal: Close < lower band → oversold → potential rebound.

Sell Signal: Close > upper band → overbought → potential pullback.

ADX (Average Directional Index)

Definition: Measures trend strength (0–100) without direction. Uses +DI and −DI lines.

Buy Signal: +DI crosses above −DI with ADX > 20 → strong bullish trend.

Sell Signal: −DI crosses above +DI with ADX > 20 → strong bearish trend.

ATR (Average True Range & Volatility)

Definition: Measures volatility via True Range: TR = max(High−Low, |High−PrevClose|, |Low−PrevClose|).

Logic: ATR% indicates price movement volatility. Not directional but helps assess risk.

Relative Strength vs S&P500

Definition: Performance relative to S&P500 over 10-day rolling window.

Logic: Positive → outperforming market; Negative → underperforming.

Fundamentals

  • PE Ratio: Price/Earnings. Low → potential value buy; high → caution/sell.
  • Revenue Growth: Positive → supports bullish setup; negative → caution.
  • Beta: Volatility vs market. Beta < 1.5 → moderate risk; > 1.5 → higher risk.
  • 52-week High / Low: Price relative to yearly range; helps spot overbought/oversold zones.

Swing Score (0–10)

Definition: Combines momentum, trend, volatility, and fundamentals into a single score.

Components & Logic:

  • RSI oversold → +1
  • MACD bullish crossover → +1
  • MA20 > MA50 → +1
  • ADX > 20 → +1
  • Price < Lower Bollinger Band → +1
  • OBV rising with price → +1
  • PE < 20 → +1
  • Revenue Growth > 5% → +1
  • Beta < 1.5 → +1
  • Price < 52-week High by >10% → +1

Score interpretation:

  • 7–10 → strong bullish setup (green)
  • 4–6 → moderate (yellow)
  • 0–3 → weak (gray)

Sell / Bearish Signals

Sell Score (0–10): Higher score → higher probability of bearish movement.

Components & Logic:

  • RSI overbought (RSI > 65) → +1
  • MACD bearish crossover → +1
  • MA20 < MA50 → +1
  • ADX > 20 and −DI > +DI → +1
  • Price > Upper Bollinger Band → +1
  • OBV falling while price is flat/rising → +1
  • PE high or negative fundamentals → +1
  • Revenue Growth negative → +1
  • Beta > 1.5 → +1
  • High Volatility % (> 5%) → +1

Interpretation: Higher sell score → stronger bearish signal, potential market reversal.
