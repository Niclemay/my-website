Technical & Fundamental Indicators Help (Detailed)

RSI (Relative Strength Index)

Definition: RSI measures the speed and magnitude of price movements to identify overbought or oversold conditions. It ranges from 0 to 100.

Calculation: RSI = 100 - (100 / (1 + RS)), where RS = average gains / average losses over 14 periods (days).

Logic in swing trading:

  • RSI < 35 → oversold → potential buy (short-term rebound likely)
  • RSI > 65 → overbought → caution or potential sell

MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)

Definition: MACD shows momentum by comparing short-term EMA (12-day) to long-term EMA (26-day).

Buy Signal Logic: MACD line crossing above Signal line (9-day EMA of MACD) → bullish momentum.

Interpretation: Useful for spotting trend reversals and momentum shifts in swing trading.

Moving Averages (MA20 & MA50)

Definition: Simple moving averages smooth price data to identify trends over time.

Buy Signal Logic:

  • MA20 crosses above MA50 (Golden Cross) → potential uptrend → buy signal
  • MA20 crosses below MA50 (Death Cross) → potential downtrend → sell signal

Volume

Definition: Volume shows the number of shares/contracts traded daily.

Logic: Volume spikes (> 1.5 × 20-day average) confirm price moves or trend breakouts, supporting trading decisions.

Bollinger Bands

Definition: Bollinger Bands consist of a 20-day SMA (middle band) and two standard deviation bands (upper & lower) to measure volatility.

Buy Signal Logic: Closing below the lower band may indicate oversold conditions → potential entry point. Conversely, closing above the upper band may indicate overbought conditions.

ADX (Average Directional Index)

Definition: ADX measures trend strength (0–100) without indicating direction. Calculated using +DI and −DI lines.

Buy Signal Logic: +DI crossing above −DI with ADX > 20 → strong bullish trend → favorable for swing trading.

ATR (Average True Range & Volatility)

Definition: ATR measures volatility by averaging True Ranges:

  • TR = max(High−Low, |High−PreviousClose|, |Low−PreviousClose|)
  • ATR = 14-day moving average of TR

Logic: ATR itself does not indicate direction. It helps traders evaluate risk and position sizing:

  • Low ATR% → stable conditions → potentially safer entry
  • High ATR% → highly volatile → higher risk

Relative Strength vs S&P500

Definition: Measures performance relative to S&P500 over a 10-day rolling window.

Calculation: Rel_Strength = (Stock_Close / S&P500_Close).pct_change().rolling(10).mean()

Logic: Positive → stock is outperforming market, stronger momentum. Negative → underperforming, weaker momentum.

Fundamentals

  • PE Ratio: Price/Earnings. Lower may indicate undervaluation.
  • Revenue Growth: YoY growth. Positive supports bullish setups.
  • Beta: Volatility relative to market. Beta < 1.5 → manageable risk.
  • 52-week High / Low: Price relative to yearly range. Helps spot overbought/oversold zones.

Swing Score (0–10)

Definition: Combines momentum, trend, volatility, and fundamentals to assess attractiveness for swing trading.

Components & Logic:

  • RSI oversold → +1 if RSI < 40
  • MACD bullish crossover → +1
  • MA20 above MA50 → +1
  • ADX > 20 → +1
  • Price < Lower Bollinger Band → +1
  • PE < 20 → +1
  • Revenue Growth > 5% → +1
  • Beta < 1.5 → +1
  • Price < 52-week High by >10% → +1
  • Volatility % < 5% → +1

Score interpretation:

  • 7–10 → strong setup (green)
  • 4–6 → moderate (yellow)
  • 0–3 → weak (gray)
