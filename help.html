Technical & Fundamental Indicators Help (Detailed)

This page explains the indicators used in the Market Indicators + Fundamentals Report, including the logic behind each one and how it contributes to the Swing Score.

Generated on: 2025-10-06 21:51:13

RSI (Relative Strength Index)

Definition: RSI measures the speed and magnitude of recent price changes to evaluate overbought or oversold conditions. Values oscillate between 0 and 100.

Calculation: RSI = 100 - (100 / (1 + RS)), where RS = average gains / average losses over 14 periods.

Logic in swing trading: If RSI is low (<35), it indicates the asset may be oversold and a short-term rebound is likely, providing a potential entry point.

MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)

Definition: MACD shows the relationship between two EMAs (12-day and 26-day). It highlights changes in momentum.

Buy Signal Logic: When the MACD line crosses above the Signal line (9-day EMA of MACD), it indicates a bullish momentum shift.

Moving Averages (MA20 & MA50)

Definition: Simple moving averages smooth price data to identify the trend over time.

Buy Signal Logic: When short-term MA20 crosses above long-term MA50 (Golden Cross), it indicates an emerging uptrend.

Volume

Definition: Volume shows how many shares/contracts are traded in a period.

Logic: Volume spikes (>1.5×20-day average) confirm the validity of a price move or trend breakout.

Bollinger Bands

Definition: Bollinger Bands consist of a 20-day SMA (middle band) and two standard deviation bands (upper and lower) that measure volatility.

Buy Signal Logic: When price closes below the lower band, it may indicate an oversold condition and a potential rebound.

ADX (Average Directional Index)

Definition: ADX measures trend strength (not direction) based on the difference between +DI and −DI lines.

Buy Signal Logic: +DI crossing above −DI with ADX > 20 signals a strong bullish trend suitable for swing trades.

ATR (Average True Range & Volatility)

Definition: ATR measures volatility using the average of true ranges (current high − low, or relative to previous close).

Logic: Swing traders use ATR as a measure of price movement risk. Low ATR% suggests stable conditions suitable for entering positions.

Relative Strength vs S&P500

Definition: Measures how a stock is performing relative to the S&P500 index over the past 10 days.

Calculation: Rel_Strength = (Stock_Close / S&P500_Close).pct_change().rolling(10).mean()

Logic:

  • Positive values → Stock is outperforming the market, indicating relative strength.
  • Negative values → Underperforming the market, weaker momentum.
  • This helps select stocks that are leading the market in a swing trading setup.

Fundamentals

  • PE Ratio: Price/Earnings ratio. Lower values may indicate undervaluation.
  • Revenue Growth: Year-over-year revenue growth. Positive growth supports bullish setups.
  • Beta: Measures stock volatility relative to the market. Beta < 1.5 is generally preferred for swing trading.
  • 52-week High / Low: Current price relative to yearly range. Helps identify overbought/oversold conditions.

Swing Score (0–10)

Definition: Composite score combining momentum, trend, volatility, and fundamental metrics to assess attractiveness for swing trading.

Components & Logic:

  • RSI oversold → +1 if RSI < 40
  • MACD bullish crossover → +1 if MACD > Signal line
  • MA20 above MA50 → +1
  • ADX > 20 → +1 (strong trend)
  • Price < Lower Bollinger Band → +1
  • Reasonable valuation (PE < 20) → +1
  • Revenue growth > 5% → +1
  • Beta < 1.5 → +1 (manageable volatility)
  • Price < 52-week high by >10% → +1
  • Volatility % < 5% → +1

Higher scores (7–10) indicate strong swing-trading opportunities (green), moderate scores (4–6) are neutral (yellow), and low scores (0–3) indicate weak setups (gray).
